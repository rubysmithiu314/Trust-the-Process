Oak Foundation Case Study

How to Play

You are the director of a nonprofit.
Your goal: survive a 30-Turn Fiscal Cycle and build programs.
Your Final CIU Score (Community Impact Units) = Budget + (Programs × 10).
The budget goal is 70 — you can surpass it.
Compare with classmates during debrief.

Each turn:
1. Hit Gain 1 Budget — collect income. Each time you gain budget, a new turn begins.
2. Hit Draw Card — draw your next card.
3. Optionally apply to a foundation.
4. If you now have 6 cards, discard one to get back to 5.

Traditional Foundation modeled at 20% approval (GrantWatch, 2025). Oak Foundation modeled at 35–80% — reflecting how established funder relationships improve success rates (Funding for Good, 2020).

Debrief Questions:

• How did juggling restricted vs. unrestricted funding shape your strategy? Looking at this through the lens of power, how do restricted funds limit a nonprofit's agency and its overall effectiveness?

• The trust ladder rewarded you with lower transaction costs as you built a relationship. How does this shift our traditional understanding of grantee accountability? Does decreasing paperwork mean decreasing accountability, or is it just shifting the power balance?

• In the game, applying for grants cost you action cards (time) and a portion of your Budget. If the transaction costs of applying are high, why do foundations still require them? Are they actually measuring a nonprofit's effectiveness, or are these hoops just about maintaining the foundation's own legitimacy?
