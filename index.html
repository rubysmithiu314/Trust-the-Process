Oak Foundation Case Study

How to Play

You lead a nonprofit. Each turn: gain 1 Budget, then draw a card. Apply to foundations to earn programs. Survive 30 turns.

Win Condition: Your Final CIU Score = Budget + (Programs × 10). CIU = Community Impact Units — financial health + programmatic reach combined. Compare with classmates at debrief.

Traditional Foundation accepts 🏥 Health, 📚 Education, 🏠 Housing → gives 7 Restricted Budget (programs only).
Oak Foundation accepts 🌿 Env & Climate, ✊ Human Rights, 🏠 Housing → gives 7 Unrestricted Budget + 🌟 multi-year bonus at game end. Trust builds with each approval.

🏠 Housing is the only program both foundations accept. You can apply to both at once — 1 Housing card, 3 actions, 3 Budget, two rolls.

Approval rates based on GrantWatch (2025): foundations fund 15–30% of applicants. Established relationships improve success to 80%+ (Funding for Good, 2020).

Your organization ran out of budget and couldn't absorb the crisis.

This mirrors reality: thousands of nonprofits close each year because one unexpected expense — a crisis card — hits when reserves are at zero. Restricted funding from the Traditional Foundation couldn't help you here, because it can only be used for program maintenance, not operational emergencies.

The lesson: Unrestricted funding from trust-based funders like Oak Foundation creates the buffer that keeps organizations alive. Restricted funding keeps programs running. You need both.
